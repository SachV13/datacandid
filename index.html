Daily Time Spent on Social Media
Global average daily time grew from 90 minutes in 2012 to 150 minutes peak in 2022, then began a slow decline as user fatigue emerged. TikTok now dominates individual app time at 55 min/day.
Global average daily minutes (2012–2025)
Average daily minutes per platform (2025)
TikTok users average 23 hours 28 minutes per month — more than any other single platform. Users now average 6.75 different social platforms per month. Despite user fatigue in Western markets, global average remains stable at 2h 24 min/day in 2025.

Before ChatGPT (Nov 2022), AI-generated social content was near zero. In just 2 years, AI posts on Facebook jumped 670%+. 71% of all social media images are now AI-generated. Only 3.6% of social media professionals don't use AI.

Organic Engagement Collapse
Facebook's organic reach dropped 98% in 13 years — from 16% of followers in 2012 to 0.2% in 2025. As AI floods feeds and algorithms throttle organic content to push ads, real human engagement is at an all-time low across every platform.

Authenticity & Consumer Trust
Public trust in AI companies has dropped 8 points since 2021. Nearly 60% of consumers now doubt the authenticity of online content. Over half reduce their engagement when they suspect AI-generated content.
Consumer trust & authenticity doubt over time (%)
2024 Consumer sentiment snapshot
The authenticity paradox: In 2023, 56% of consumers preferred AI content before knowing it was AI. Once told, only 26% still preferred it. Trust in AI influencers fell from 34% (2023) to 26% (2024) — yet platforms continue prioritising AI content because it maximises engagement.
The Advertising Engine
Every algorithm decision ultimately serves ad revenue. Social media ad spend grew from $51B in 2017 to $220B in 2025. Meta alone captures $134B+ annually. Algorithms don't optimise for human connection — they optimise for advertiser return.

The BeReal Experiment
BeReal proved humans crave authenticity — and proved they won't stay for it without algorithmic dopamine loops. It peaked at 73M users in 2022 before collapsing to under 10M by 2024. Every major platform cloned its dual-camera feature and moved on.
